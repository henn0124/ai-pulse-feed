In the last 24–48 hours, the strongest signal from Tier 1 AI-dev accounts is a shift from **AI as assistant** to **AI as full workflow surface**.

## What happened

### 1) Cursor is expanding beyond its own editor
@cursor_ai announced Cursor availability in **JetBrains IDEs via the Agent Client Protocol** and rolled out **MCP Apps** that let agents render interactive UIs inside conversations.

**Why it matters:** this reduces editor lock-in and pushes agent workflows toward a protocol-based ecosystem (ACP/MCP), where capability portability becomes a competitive edge.

### 2) Simon Willison highlighted two converging themes
@simonw posted about:
- Ongoing **agentic engineering patterns** (including anti-patterns like shipping unreviewed large PRs)
- A potentially important ecosystem event around the **Qwen 3.5 open-weight family** and reported leadership departures

**Why it matters:** we’re seeing rapid capability gains and rapid governance/operational turbulence at the same time. Teams that scale review and security discipline will outperform teams that only scale output.

### 3) Milan Milanović emphasized org-level productivity friction
@milan_milanovic focused on **context switching costs** and shared commentary on AI tooling adoption and reliability discussions.

**Why it matters:** even with better models, delivery speed is still capped by human process debt (interruptions, review bottlenecks, tool sprawl).

## Cross-account read

The near-term winner in AI dev tooling likely won’t just have the “best model.” It will be the platform that combines:
1. **Protocol interoperability** (ACP/MCP)
2. **Review + security guardrails** for agent-generated code
3. **Low-friction team workflows** that preserve deep-focus engineering time

## Notable gap

No major fresh posts from @karpathy in this 24–48h window, so today’s signal is driven more by product surface expansion and engineering-process governance than by new model-training commentary.
